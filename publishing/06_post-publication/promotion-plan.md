# Promotion plan — first 4 weeks

The 4 weeks after the DOI goes live matter most for early visibility. Use the `highlights.md` from `../02_pre-submission/highlights.md` as the messaging base.

## Week 0 (publication day)

- [ ] **MDPI listing:** verify article appears correctly on https://www.mdpi.com/journal/energies/ and on the section A1 page.
- [ ] **ORCID:** confirm Crossref auto-pushed the record (V.T. and K.G.M.K.).
- [ ] **SciProfiles:** add the article to V.T. and K.G.M.K. profiles.
- [ ] **ResearchGate:** upload the published PDF (CC-BY allows this); link DOI.
- [ ] **Personal/lab website:** add a publications page entry.
- [ ] **Internal lab Slack / Teams / mailing list:** short announcement.

## Week 1

- [ ] **LinkedIn** post by V.T. and K.G.M.K. — re-use highlights, link DOI, tag co-authors and the lab.
- [ ] **X / Bluesky / Mastodon** thread — short version of highlights.
- [ ] **Mailing list (if any) for the smart-grid / energy informatics community.**
- [ ] **Email to relevant conference Special Issue editors** flagging the paper.

## Week 2

- [ ] **Conference / workshop callouts**: if the work fits an upcoming CFP, reference it explicitly.
- [ ] **Departmental newsletter / institutional press office** notified.
- [ ] **Direct emails** to ~10 researchers whose work we cite or whose work overlaps — short, personal note + DOI.

## Week 3–4

- [ ] **Blog post** on the lab/personal blog distilling the contribution for a wider audience.
- [ ] **Talk / lightning slot** at any reading group, internal seminar, or local meetup.
- [ ] **Cross-link from related repos** (e.g. KIOZE GitHub repos) to the DOI.

## Ethics

- Never present the paper as more than it is. The highlights should match the abstract; the social posts should match the highlights.
- No reviewer-bashing, no journal-bashing if the process was difficult.
